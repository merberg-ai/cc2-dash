Camera Relay / Stream Protection

Keeps cc2-dash-lite to one upstream camera connection per printer, then fans that stream out to dashboard browsers, snapshots, watchdog vision, and the embedded portal.

Enable camera relayRecommended. Browser clients and Portal AI read from the local relay instead of dogpiling the printer camera.
Start relay on bootOpens one upstream camera connection as soon as the backend starts, so watchdog vision has a frame ready.
Max client FPSLimits how fast each browser client receives frames from the relay. Lower is kinder to phones and Pi CPUs.
Stale frame thresholdSeconds before the relay marks the camera stale and begins reconnect/recovery behavior.
Upstream timeoutsConnect timeout and read timeout in seconds for the single printer camera connection.
Rewrite stock portal camera URLsAttempts to redirect the embedded Elegoo portal camera references through the local relay instead of printer :8080.
Fallback to direct camera for visionUsually leave this off. Turning it on lets Portal AI hit the printer directly if the relay has no frame, which can reintroduce connection pileups.

Portal AI 🤖

Explainable failure detection using printer telemetry first, with optional server-side camera frame checks through your LAN Ollama vision model.

Enable Portal AITurns the dashboard AI risk card on or off.
Background watchdogKeeps Portal AI monitoring from the backend service even when no browser is open.
Background check intervalSeconds between watchdog checks. Lower is more responsive; higher is quieter.
Log state/risk changes onlyPrevents the watchdog from spamming the log every check.
Minimum watchdog log levelOnly write background AI log entries at or above this risk level.
Telemetry rulesPrinter error state, MQTT freshness, temperatures, filament sensor, and progress movement.
Camera health hintsUses printer-reported camera availability where available.
Ollama vision monitoringSamples printer camera frames from the backend and asks your LAN Ollama vision model for a conservative second opinion.
